March Mat-ness

By Thomas Munson | This weekend men, women and children across America will follow the madness on the hardwood. But, when their brackets are inevitably busted, there is another tournament to enjoy. It has not one bracket, but ten. Here are the four Quakers who will try to grapple their way to the top of NCAA wrestling glory at the world’s most famous arena:

Casey Kent

After missing his junior campaign, this is the 174-pounders second shot at glory. A January victory over the nation’s top grappler vaulted him up in the polls. He will attempt to erase his poor showing at the conference tournament.

Lorenzo Thomas

The 2014 All-American is the most accomplished member of the bunch. A crowded field with familiar foes awaits the 184-pound senior. He will need to avenge old losses and repeat former victories to reach the podium again.

3rd trip to NCAA 4th place at 2016 EIWA tournament 2015 1st team all-Ivy 2015 NWCA All-American

Caleb Richardson

It’s his third trip in as many years to the big stage, but he has just one NCAA tournament match win to his name. The 133-pound junior hopes to breakout this year in the Big Apple.

May Bethea

It’s not the first time a Bethea has made it this far for Penn, but it’s the first time May has. Still, nothing intimidates the sophomore 157-pounder and he has the wins to prove it.
