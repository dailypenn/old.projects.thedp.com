Rothman | The time for a tournament run is now

By Tommy Rothman

After a year away from the throne, the Penn women's basketball returned to the top of the Ivy League last season. I'm not used to having high expectations for sports teams I follow (Let's Go Knicks!) but it's hard not to let one's thoughts jump ahead to the NCAA tournament with this team.

Why? Because they were just there. Not just the program, but the actual roster. The Red and Blue didn't lose a single rotation player to graduation this past offseason. So if the other Ivy League teams don't have a way to beat this Quakers squad (and they certainly didn't appear to last year), they better figure one out fast.

Stop me if this sounds familiar: The Quakers have the two best bigs in the Ivy league, a great backcourt filled with guards who can shoot and get the ball to the post, a lockdown defense, and the best coach in the conference.

Now, Penn certainly didn't run away with the Ivy League title last season. Princeton stuck with the Quakers and fell just short in a thrilling season-finale that would have forced a one-game playoff. Even so, the Tigers earned an at-large bid to the NCAA Tournament, making the Ivy League, for the first time ever, a two-bid conference.

Furthermore, getting to the NCAA Tournament is no longer a matter of being the best team all season long. Even with an undefeated run through the conference, the Quakers would have to earn their ticket to the Big Dance by winning the first-ever Ivy League Tournament. Having the best record would help, but the team would still be two wins away.

But it's hard to imagine anybody other than Penn cutting down the nets this season. While Penn returns almost wholly intact from a season ago, Princeton has graduated several major contributors. Unless a third team can quickly bridge a very large gap, or pull off a huge upset in the Ivy tournament, this is Penn's conference.

But winning the NCAA berth isn't enough, not this year. A season ago, Penn just missed out on its first-ever tournament win, hanging tough with Washington before the Huskies pulled away in the fourth.

And if the Quakers had gotten that one win, more might have been on the way. Washington was the story of the tournament (aside from the nightly UConn blowout), fighting its way to the Final Four behind Chantel Osahor, who became an internet sensation with her unorthodox shooting style, and Kelsey Plum, who will soon be a top-five pick (and perhaps the first overall) in the WNBA draft.

For most of the game between Penn and the Huskies, the two sides looked to be evenly matched. So there's no reason the Red and Blue couldn't have been the Cinderella team making a deep tournament run.

Can this Penn team shock the world this time around? Maybe. A deep tournament run certainly isn't likely, or else it wouldn't be very shocking.

But the program's first-ever tournament win should be very much in play. If nothing else, the Quakers will get another chance, at least. It would be truly shocking if they didn't.
